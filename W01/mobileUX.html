PC Market reached it's peak in 2011.

New paradigm shift started in 2007/2011. Which was mobile.

    Intro
  • Mobile device turnover is about every 2 years.
  • Pleathora of devices to design for now.
  • Single device per family to 2-3 devices per family
  • Simple guideline is that whatever do design for mobile first.
  • Just because it is responsive, it doesn't mean it is design for mobile.
  • Responsive retrofitting is the first step, but eventually mobile first is the way to go.
  • Mobile first means we are future proofing our products.
  • reqire our brains
    • People don't carry laptops anymore.
    • start small and scale up. This ensures the focus is on the content.
    • Things grow better than they shrink.
    • Mobile is not a fad.
    Understanding Users
  • Be There - be present on Mobile
  • Be Useful - don't make users hunt for what they want.
  • Be Quick - reduce cognitive load. A single tap causes friction.
  • you can do something automatically that doesn't need to be done manually.
  • reachability is super important. They should have to be able to do stuff without moving there hand.(primary Controls)
  • Speed is super important. 1.2 a second causes google 20% drop in traffic.
  • More poeple have slow internet compared to faster internet. So lean, fast applications are valuable.
  • Reduce images or use svg/webfonts
  • optimize and minify css and JS files. it makes a huge difference.
  • GZIP files to eliminate unnecessary files.
    Strategic Menu Design
  • Primary Controls - We need to care about the users needs
  • Menus are generally at the top, outside of the main tapping areas. Move the menu to the bottom.
  • Facebook put there menu on the bottom on the iPhone. Addresses reachability - which is percieved as a faster app. Improved engagement and increased satisfaction.
  • We should always strive for amazing.
  • The Hamburger menu shouldn't be used anymore because many people aren't familiar with it. hamburger plus Menu is the best option. It makes it look like a button. Both should be there WITH a border. IT leads to an understanding of the Hamburger menu down the road.
    Intuitive and Mobile Friendly Forms
  • focus on Mbile forces simplified solutions.
  • Forms these days ask for too much.
  • use float labels and placeholders instead of labels
  • Remove unneccessary forms.
  • Preferences determine what the form looks like.
  • When forms do need many pieces of data, Paginate it. But please include a progress indicator. Mobile data entry is tough. Use autofill when it is feasible.
  • Passwords on a mobile device is awful.
  • Password masking is dumb. Show the password and allopw it to be hidden if they want.
  • Card information is much easier when you auto advance to the next field. Provide spaces to make it easier to read
  • make sure the proper keyboard is displayed
  • use an anchor tag for phone numbers to make the user get to tap rather than copy and paste, etc.
  • The easier it is the more money you make.
    Mobile Advertising
  • End users, not technologies shape the market.
  • Ad units should not use desktop advertising sizes
  • pop-up ads are a no.
  • Content first. Ads should not get in the way of content.
  • Ads should be simple and still effective.
  • video ads result in higher conversion rates.
  • ads can be effective when they are fun and creative.
  • Don't use pop-ups and pop-overs., keep it simple and sleek, unobtrusive, and cotnent first.
  • Empathy is huge. Pissed off users do not equal conversions are revenue.
